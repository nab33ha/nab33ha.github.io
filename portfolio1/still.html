A Beautiful Mess

Medium: Charcoal

An observational sketch I made of my washroom sink, which is typical to a scene of any other teenage girl’s bathroom or room right before she leaves for an outing. A tangled spread of cosmetics to beautify ourselves is what we leave behind. It is an instance of a beautiful outcome emerging from an ugly mess. The face scrub, razor, and perfume represent a person’s need to be perfect in all dimensions- sight, touch, and smell, but not mind. The hurried application of a range of products kept on the sink is symbolic to the vigorous efforts made by a person to mask their internal distress in order to paint a pretty picture for the society. I used Charcoal for this gray-scale still-life to add a heavy, dark and strong tonal theme, depicting the colorless catalyst behind a vibrant outcome, which represents the reality of the immense pressure on a person to be presentable at all times, even if it means overlooking the internal mess.
